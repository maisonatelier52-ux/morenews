Federico Valverde Hat-Trick Powers Real Madrid to 3-0 Win Over Manchester City

Sports › Federico Valverde Hat-Trick Powers Real Madrid to 3-0 Win Over Manchester City

By Callum Fraser

Published Date: 12 March 2026

Federico Valverde produced one of the finest individual performances of his career as Real Madrid thrashed Manchester City 3-0 in the first leg of their UEFA Champions League last-16 tie at the Santiago Bernabéu. The Uruguayan captain delivered a remarkable first-half hat-trick in just 42 minutes, firmly establishing himself in the club's history while leaving Pep Guardiola's side with a mountain to climb ahead of the second leg.

Valverde's three goals showcased both his technical quality and his predatory instincts in front of goal, coming against a Manchester City defence that was repeatedly caught out of position. His first arrived after he collected a long ball from Thibaut Courtois, shrugged off a defender, and rounded Gianluigi Donnarumma to finish from a tight angle. The second followed a defensive error drawn out by Vinícius Júnior's direct running, with Valverde driving low into the far corner with his weaker foot. The third was the pick of the three — a moment of sublime technique, lifting the ball delicately over Marc Guéhi before volleying into the net to complete a magnificent hat-trick.

Scoring three goals in the first half against a side of City's calibre is an extraordinary achievement at this level, and it underlined just how important Valverde has become to Carlo Ancelotti's team, especially during a period when several key players have been unavailable through injury.

Leadership Display in the Absence of Madrid Stars

Real Madrid entered the match facing a challenging situation, with their squad depleted by a cluster of significant injuries. Kylian Mbappé, Rodrygo, and Jude Bellingham were all ruled out, forcing Ancelotti to rely heavily on those available to step into the void. Valverde, captaining the side in the absence of the usual starters, did not merely fill a role — he dominated the entire first half.

The emotional significance of the performance was not lost on those watching from the stands. Bellingham was visibly stunned as the third goal went in, while Mbappé was seen celebrating enthusiastically at the final whistle, both players clearly aware of what their teammate had achieved in their absence.

Throughout a difficult season in which he has been asked to play multiple positions to cover for others — from central midfield to the wing and even at right-back on occasion — Valverde has met every demand without complaint. His relentless work ethic, his intensity, and his ability to influence matches in both halves of the pitch were all on full display as Madrid recorded a decisive first-leg victory.

Tactical Gamble Leaves Manchester City Exposed

Pep Guardiola's tactical decisions came under scrutiny after the full-time whistle. City set up in an attacking 4-2-2-2 formation, placing significant responsibility on the pace and directness of Jérémy Doku, Savinho, and Erling Haaland to generate a threat going forward. While the approach created some early openings, it also left the defence dangerously exposed whenever Real Madrid transitioned quickly.

Valverde exploited those spaces repeatedly, finding pockets of room between the lines and punishing defensive hesitation with clinical efficiency. Guardiola introduced Tijjani Reijnders after the interval in an attempt to add more defensive solidity in midfield, but the contest had long since been settled.

City's fragility at the back, combined with the home side's ruthless finishing, transformed what had been expected to be a tightly contested European tie into a comprehensive success for the Spanish champions.

A Rare and Historic Champions League Feat

The significance of Valverde's hat-trick extends well beyond a single dominant performance. In the 26 Champions League matches he had played before this encounter, he had failed to score on a single occasion. Against City, he matched his previous tally for the entire competition in the space of one half of football.

The achievement also placed Valverde in rare historical company. He became only the second player ever to score a first-half hat-trick against an English club in Champions League history, joining Lionel Messi, who accomplished the feat for Barcelona against Arsenal in 2010. Measured against any statistical or historical benchmark, the display ranks among the most extraordinary individual contributions seen in a knockout-stage match in recent memory.

The Underrated Engine of Real Madrid

Despite arriving at Real Madrid from Nacional in 2017, Valverde has long operated somewhat outside the spotlight occupied by the club's more celebrated names. Born and raised in Montevideo, he has spoken in the past about how a modest upbringing shaped his mentality and his commitment to hard work — qualities that have defined his career at the very highest level.

He is the kind of player whose contribution does not always translate neatly into headlines. The ground covered in a match, the pressing triggered, the defensive responsibilities fulfilled alongside the attacking ones — these are the qualities that teammates and coaches have always valued most. Trent Alexander-Arnold, who trains alongside him at the Bernabéu, once described him as the most underrated footballer on the planet.

On this occasion, there was nothing understated about his contribution. His first senior hat-trick pushed Real Madrid firmly towards the quarter-finals and served as an emphatic reminder that, when the occasion demands it, Federico Valverde is capable of producing moments that belong to the very highest tier of the sport.
